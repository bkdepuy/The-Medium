# The Medium — A Foundational Theory of Reality

## Overview

The Medium is a foundational framework for understanding the nature of
reality at its most primitive level. It proposes that the substrate of
reality is a single infinite undifferentiated thing — the Medium —
possessing intrinsic distinction as an eternal property. Physical
reality, space, time, and locality are not primitive. They emerge from
the Medium through a process of differentiation that culminates in
self-sustaining structure.

The framework's foundational claims:

- The Medium always is and always was. It has no beginning and no end.
- Time is not primitive. What physics calls time is transition — a
  change from one state of differentiation to another.
- Locality is not primitive. Location does not exist until
  differentiation, propagating at the limit c, produces self-sustaining
  structure. Space begins there.
- The existing mathematical frameworks of physics — quantum mechanics
  and general relativity — are expected to emerge as valid descriptions
  of differentiated structure behavior once locality is established.
  Their incompatibility is a property of the approximations, not of
  the reality beneath them.
